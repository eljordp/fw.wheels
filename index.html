Wheel Fitment FAQ

Quick answers for the details that decide whether a wheel setup actually fits.

How do I know what wheel size fits my car?

Fitment depends on diameter, width, bolt pattern, offset, center bore, tire size, brake clearance, and the stance you want. Send your year, make, model, trim, and goal if you want help checking it.

What does offset mean?

Offset controls how far the wheel sits in or out from the hub. A lower offset usually pushes the wheel outward; the right number depends on your vehicle, wheel width, suspension, and tire size.

Do I need hub rings or special lug nuts?

Many aftermarket wheels need hub rings, spline lug nuts, lug bolts, or different valve stems. The hardware depends on your vehicle and the wheel specs.

Can I order a staggered setup?

Yes. Staggered setups can look aggressive, but they need extra fitment checks for width, offset, tire sizing, and clearance.
